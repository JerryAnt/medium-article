How I changed my life

becoming a web dev

Back in 2020 a new virus spread all over the world. Working in proximity with other people was getting intense. With all the restrictions and government measures, I really felt the need to insure my safety and the safety of my family.

Working remote?! That is an idea...

At that time, I had just made a change of path in my career. I was working in the Construction industry. Being from a marketing background, I had felt the curiosity of working in a radically different environment plus I had just bought a house and I wanted to be able to build projects out of it. I also wanted to work out my body as sitting on a chair all day from 9to5 was not improving my shape

The pandemics and the risks towards my safety at work made me think twice about my personal goals and career expectations.

In the same time, a friend of mine who was very sick was looking for a medium to express and share his feelings. I had been working as a marketing consultant I had some experience in the maintenance of some websites. I built him a website on wordpress. Nothing too fancy of course but at least he had the tools he was looking for and felt very happy despite his illness.

While I was doing the website, I had a lot of funs looking for tools, touching some html again, building the structure of the website.

I thought that maybe developing websites would be a great way to move towards something I had loved without realizing it soon enough.

It would completely satisfy my needs of projects building, curiosity, fun with architectures and I would even be able to do that remotely

My wife told me about Le Wagon and how fun the experience seem to look and also the employability rate looked awesome

So, I asked for an interview with the school to know more about the program

But I was not convinced

I let it soak and applied 6 months later

What a fun experience indeed....

After 6 months of intensive learning and applying concepts I can say that I am part of the dev team

Of course I am still a newbie but I got concepts in mind, I code everyday and whenever I am stuck, I know where to look for information
